Clinical Attachment Programme

Frequently Asked Questions

Key information for International Medical Graduates considering the NHS primary care observership at Hoyland Medical Practice.

Is there an age requirement?

Learners must be international medical graduates who graduated from a university listed in the World Directory of Medical Schools. There is no age requirement.

Can I complete an attachment longer than 4 weeks?

At present, the programme is offered as a 4-week NHS primary care observership.

Do I need to be in the UK to apply?

No. You can register interest from outside the UK, but you must ensure you have the necessary visa or right to visit the UK.

Can Hoyland Medical Practice sponsor my visa?

No. The practice cannot sponsor or assist with visa applications and cannot provide visa sponsorship letters.

Is accommodation available?

Accommodation is not provided, but the team can signpost candidates toward local accommodation options if needed.

Do I need a DBS check?

You may be asked to provide either a UK DBS certificate or an overseas police clearance certificate, with translation if required.

Will I have a badge?

Yes. On your first day, you will be issued a badge displaying your full name and position as a Clinical Observer.

What is the dress code?

Smart-casual and professional attire is recommended.

Can I attend on weekends or evenings?

No. Attachments are offered during primary care working hours, Monday-Friday, 08:00-18:00, on set cohort dates and excluding bank holidays.

Why is the fee non-refundable?

The fee covers administrative and placement-related costs. Fees are non-refundable once a place has been confirmed, but candidates can avail of another cohort in the next 12 months, subject to availability.

Will I have an induction?

Yes. Candidates receive an induction on the first day, including orientation to the practice, observer role and relevant governance requirements.

Is there somewhere to buy lunch?

You may bring a packed lunch. Nearby places to purchase lunch are available in Hoyland and the surrounding area.

Will I receive a certificate?

Yes. A certificate confirming attendance is provided after successful completion of the attachment.

Can I complete audits during my attachment?

Portfolio, audit or quality improvement exposure may be available depending on practice capacity and supervision.

Will I receive a reference at the end?

References are discretionary and depend on attendance, professionalism and performance during the programme. Completion does not guarantee a reference.
